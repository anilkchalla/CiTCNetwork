About the Conference

Where CRISPR Education Meets Community

CRISPR in the Classroom (CiTC) is a national gathering of undergraduate educators who are transforming how CRISPR–Cas9 gene-editing technology is taught, researched, and experienced in labs and classrooms across the country.

What Is CiTC?

CRISPR in the Classroom is a network of educators, researchers, and course designers who integrate CRISPR technology into undergraduate biology education — through lectures, lab modules, Course-Based Undergraduate Research Experiences (CUREs), and interdisciplinary projects. This conference is your chance to showcase your work, learn from peers, and collectively shape the future of CRISPR pedagogy.

🔗 Join the CiTC Network on QUBES Hub →

About RCN-UBE

The Research Coordination Network for Undergraduate Biology Education (RCN-UBE) is an NSF-funded initiative dedicated to building a robust, collaborative community of practice around innovative undergraduate biology teaching. The CiTC conference is a flagship event of the network, bringing together its most influential educators for an intensive weekend of talks, demos, poster sessions, and community-building.

🔬 NSF Award #2120417

Who Should Attend?

This conference is open to any undergraduate educator who has used, taught, or developed curriculum around CRISPR technology — whether in traditional lab courses, CUREs, lecture demonstrations, or independent research with students. Graduate students and postdocs involved in undergraduate CRISPR education are also welcome.

Why Rollins College?

Nestled in the charming college town of Winter Park, Florida, Rollins College offers a beautiful lakeside campus with excellent conference facilities. Winter Park is renowned for its dining scene, walkable Park Avenue, and proximity to Orlando's cultural and entertainment options — making this the perfect setting for three days of inspiration and collaboration.

Logistics & Travel

The Nuts and Bolts

We have done our best to make travel easy and comfortable. Here is a quick overview — see the FAQ below for more detail on each topic.

✈️

Getting Here

Two airports serve Rollins College: Orlando International (MCO) — major airlines, ~30 min drive — and Sanford International (SFB) — discount carriers (Allegiant Air), ~30 min drive. Both offer Uber/Lyft and car rentals.

🏠

Lodging on Campus

Attendees stay in Lakeside Residence Hall, Rollins' nicest dorms. Apartment-style units with private rooms, private bathrooms, and a shared common space (4 people/unit). Bring your own linens.

🚗

Driving & Parking

Parking passes are available at two covered decks: Truist Parking Garage and Rollins Parking Garage. Request a pass when you register. Campus address: 1000 Holt Ave, Winter Park, FL 32789.

🍽️

Food & Dining

Meals split between the campus dining hall (most dietary restrictions accommodated) and local Winter Park restaurants. Winter Park is an acclaimed food destination — your Uber driver will have excellent recommendations.

🌦️

Weather in Florida

Expect hot, humid mornings with afternoon thunderstorms typical of Florida summer (around 3:00 PM). Pack lightweight, breathable clothing and bring an umbrella or rain jacket. Evenings are warm and pleasant.

💰

Travel Grants

A limited number of travel grants are available through NSF funding. Contact Dr. Donna Pattison (University of Houston) as soon as possible after registering to receive UH reimbursement paperwork and begin the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Your Questions, Answered

Still have questions? Reach out to the conference organizers directly.

What accommodations are available on campus?
We have reserved Lakeside Residence Hall — the nicest dorms on campus. These are apartment-style units where each person has their own private room and private bathroom, with a shared common space. Each unit houses 4 individuals. Important: You must bring your own linens. There are nearby hotels available; however, those within walking distance of campus are quite expensive and not covered by our grant. One enjoyable alternative is the Alfond Inn Art Tour, which features an extensive rotating private art collection.
What is the best way to get to Rollins College?
Orlando has two major airports: Orlando International (MCO), which handles all major airlines, and Sanford International (SFB), which serves mostly discount/à la carte carriers (Allegiant Air is their largest airline). Both MCO and SFB are approximately a 30–35-minute drive to the Rollins campus. Lyft, Uber, and taxi service are available at both airports. Car rental is also available at each airport.
Can I drive to Rollins College?
Yes! Parking passes will be available to park in one of the campus parking decks for the duration of the conference. The two options are the Truist Parking Garage and the Rollins Parking Garage. Either may be used, and both offer mostly covered parking. Request your parking pass when you register.
Can I bring my family?
Orlando is a wonderful family destination! However, we cannot house families in the on-campus conference lodging. The beach is about an hour from campus and the major theme parks are a 30–45-minute drive depending on traffic. If you wish to bring your family, we recommend having them join you in Orlando after the conference concludes on Sunday, or meeting up at another location after the event.
What will the food be like?
Meals are split between the on-campus dining service (which can accommodate most dietary restrictions) and local Winter Park restaurants. Winter Park has a well-deserved reputation as a top dining destination in the Orlando metro area — with excellent options just a short walk from campus. Orlando is also known as a foodie city with several Michelin-starred restaurants, and many of the best spots are tucked away in strip malls and off-the-beaten-path locations. Ask your Uber driver for their personal favorites!
What should I expect weather-wise?
Florida in June means hot, humid days. Summer is also rainy season — it typically stays very hot and humid until around 3:00 PM, when a significant rain shower often rolls in for up to an hour, after which conditions improve. Pack lightweight, breathable clothing, sunscreen, and bring an umbrella or compact rain jacket. The evenings are warm and perfect for exploring Winter Park.
Is travel reimbursement available?
Travel reimbursement may be available through the NSF grant. If you are attending and would like to request reimbursement, please contact Dr. Donna Pattison (University of Houston) as soon as you register so she can set you up with the UH reimbursement paperwork in a timely manner.
What is the registration deadline?
The registration deadline is May 1, 2026. This conference is now open to anyone in your professional network who works with CRISPR in undergraduate education — please share widely!
